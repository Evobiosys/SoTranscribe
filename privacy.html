Privacy

How we handle your data

Plain version: we don’t want your data on US infrastructure, we don’t use Google Analytics, Stripe, or Google Fonts, and where we host things ourselves the providers are in the EU or Switzerland. If we ever process audio for you (hosted tier), the audio stays on Austrian or Swiss servers, and you can leave with your full transcripts at any time.

What this page covers

This privacy notice covers the marketing site at sotranscribe.com. The hosted SoTranscribe service is not yet live; when it launches, a service-specific notice will be linked here. The self-host version processes audio entirely on your machine and does not send anything to us.

What we collect on this page

Server logs

The page is served by GitHub Pages (V1). Standard web server logs may be retained by the host. We do not query or aggregate them.

Analytics

None at the moment. If we add analytics, we’ll use Plausible on an EU instance — cookieless, GDPR-native, no personal data collected, no IP storage. We’ll update this notice before any tracker goes live.

Form submissions (waitlist / contact)

If you click “Join the beta waitlist”, the link opens your mail client with a pre-filled message to connect@sotranscribe.com. Email content is processed via the SoTranscribe mail infrastructure (Infomaniak, Switzerland). We use the email address only to contact you about the waitlist.

Fonts

Inter is loaded via Bunny Fonts (Slovenia, EU) — a GDPR-compliant Google Fonts mirror. No Google Fonts. No tracking by font fetch.

Why no Stripe / no US providers?

The US CLOUD Act (2018) lets US authorities compel US-jurisdiction providers to disclose stored data, even when the data is held on EU soil. Audio of meetings is among the most sensitive content a person creates — we don’t want that exposure on our chain, and we suspect you don’t either. Hence Mollie (NL) instead of Stripe, Bunny Fonts (SI) instead of Google Fonts, and a long-term commitment to migrate everything off US infrastructure.

Why Austria and Switzerland for hosting

We host transcription infrastructure in Austria and Switzerland. Both are outside the U.S. CLOUD Act. Austria adds an extra layer: under Austrian law, providers can only be compelled to release data on a targeted, judicially-warranted basis — never as part of a dragnet. That’s a stronger protection than Switzerland on this specific point, and it’s why we host in both, so you can pick.

Your rights (GDPR)

  • Access — ask what data we hold about you.
  • Erasure — ask us to delete it.
  • Portability — for the hosted tier (when live), full transcripts are exportable in Markdown + JSON. No lock-in.
  • Complaint — you can complain to your national data-protection authority. In Austria: Datenschutzbehörde.

To exercise any right, mail connect@sotranscribe.com.

Data minimisation principle

Self-host: nothing leaves your machine. Hosted (when live): we process the minimum needed to transcribe. Your audio gets transcribed and stored on your behalf; we don’t train models on it, we don’t share it, we don’t analyse it for marketing. Crown-jewel data deserves the strongest available promise.
